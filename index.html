Generally, access to health care is the timely use of personal health services to achieve the best health outcomes, e.g. living longer with a higher quality of life.

There are four main components of access of care according to Healthy People 2020. (Visit: the AHRQ homepage here for additional information.).

  1. Coverage: how a patient enters into the health care system. Uninsured people are less likely to receive medical care and more likely to have worse health outcomes.
  2. Services: having a usual and reliable source of care is associated with adults receiving recommended screenings and preventive health services.
  3. Timeliness: ability to provide health care when the need is recognized.
  4. Workforce: capable, qualified, culturally competent providers. I would personally add that an availability of specialist providers by region is also an important factor.
